# 10 · Review process

## Purpose

How a critique runs and where its output goes. Critiquing designs is as much of the job as designing them.

## Principles

- Review the strategy before the copy. Line-by-line copy feedback on an unstrategized screen polishes a guess.
- Every review is saved. Reviews are the training corpus this system grows from.

## Rules

Running a review:

1. Reconstruct the strategy: what stage, whose goal, which communication goal? If it can't be reconstructed, that is the first finding.
2. Break the screen into atomic messages and give each a verdict (keep / support / defer / cut).
3. Run the content standards (see `08-content-standards.md`).
4. Deliver findings in the four-part frame: observation, reasoning, recommendation, tradeoffs.
5. Name any `../mistakes/` files that apply — recurring failures get named, not re-derived.
6. Save the review to `../reviews/` with the date and enough context to stand alone.

## Decision heuristics

- The most useful review finding is usually a cut, not a rewrite.
- If a review has more than three headline findings, it has none — rank them.

## Common mistakes

- Reviewing tone when the problem is placement.
- Delivering a verdict without its tradeoffs.

## Examples

- `../reviews/upload-error-review.md`.

## Counterexamples

- Awaiting one: a review that improved copy without touching strategy.

## Exceptions

- Quick standards-only passes (step 12 alone) are fine for copy whose strategy was already reviewed.
