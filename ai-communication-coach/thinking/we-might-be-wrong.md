# We might be wrong

Standing doubts about our own rules. When one resolves, version the decision in `../books/09-decision-library.md`.

- **Cutting apologies entirely.** The coach cuts "we're sorry" on sight. But an apology may carry trust value that doesn't show up in "what would the user do differently?" — our test question measures action, not feeling, and reassurance is a feeling.
- **Terse at tense moments.** We hold that terse beats warm-and-wordy under stress. The failure mode (cold, evasive) is real and we may be past the line in some cases without knowing it.
- **"Inform" as a suspect goal.** The coach treats pure inform as a goal in hiding. Some information genuinely exists to build a mental model over time, with no immediate behavior change. Our framework has no good home for that.
- **One channel per message.** Redundancy across channels is how humans actually remember things. Our anti-duplication rule optimizes for per-moment attention; it may under-serve memory.
