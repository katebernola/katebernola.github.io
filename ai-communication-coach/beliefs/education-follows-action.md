# Education follows action

## Belief

Users learn better after they've committed to a task than before. Teaching works best inside an action, not ahead of it.

## Evidence

- Retirement enrollment: educational content (401(k) definitions, compound interest, tax explanations) ahead of the action was cut or deferred; the user goal was confidence to begin, not comprehension. See `../patterns/retirement-enrollment.md`.
- Payroll — awaiting the written case (see `../patterns/payroll-errors.md`).
- General observation: tips read before they matter are tips forgotten.

## Exceptions

- Legal disclosures.
- Medical decisions.
- Financial risk — anywhere an uninformed commitment could genuinely harm the user, education must come first.

## Confidence

High
