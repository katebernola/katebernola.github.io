# Upload error — before and after

From the first full framework run, 2026-07-18. Full reasoning: [../../reviews/upload-error-review.md](../../reviews/upload-error-review.md).

## Before — 49 words, 6 messages

> Oops! Something went wrong with your upload. We're really sorry about that. Please note that files over 2GB are not supported at this time. You can try again, or check out our new bulk uploader which is now available to all Pro users. Your other files were uploaded successfully.

## After the coach — 17 words, 3 messages

> **Something went wrong with your upload.**
> Files over 2GB won't upload.
> *Your other files are safe.* (helper text)

## After human review — 14 words, 3 messages

> **Files over 2GB won't upload.**
> Reduce the file size and try again.
> *Your other files are safe.* (helper text)

## What moved the needle

- Two messages cut outright ("Oops!", the apology) — neither changed what the user did next.
- The generic wrapper cut too: the specific problem is its own announcement. "Something went wrong" adds nothing once the cause is named.
- The next step made viable and complete: "try again" alone would just fail again.
- The upsell deferred to its own moment, with an owner and a review date. An upsell and an error are different message types — they never share a sentence.
- "were uploaded successfully" → "are safe": reassurance in the user's terms, at a grade-2 reading level instead of grade 12.

## The lesson

Every removal made the survivors louder. Nothing was added — the copy got better entirely by deciding, not writing. And the two passes did different jobs: the coach's rules enforced discipline; the human review supplied correctness.
