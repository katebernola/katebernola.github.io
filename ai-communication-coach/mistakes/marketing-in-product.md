# Marketing in product

## What it looks like

An upsell inside an error message. A feature pitch in a cancel flow. "We're excited to announce" anywhere the user came to do a task.

## Why it happens

- The business goal has no moment of its own, so it hitchhikes on the user's moment.
- In-product surfaces have great reach, and reach looks free on a dashboard.

## How to catch it

- Check the stage: persuasion during a moment of need or a leaving flow is the classic case.
- Read the message in the user's voice: would they say they wanted this here?

## The fix

Inside a product, users expect a tool, not a pitch. Persuasion works only when it removes a doubt, not when it adds pressure. If the upgrade genuinely solves the user's problem, frame it as the fix — after the crisis copy does its job. Otherwise, find the message a calmer moment.

## The PLG nuance

In a product-led-growth context, a free-tier user hitting a plan limit is a legitimate moment to prompt a buy-up — the upgrade really is the fix. That doesn't rescue the fused message. An upsell and an error are completely different message types needing completely different treatments: separate messages, separate framing, and the error's copy does its job first. "Check out our new…" fails not because upgrading is wrong but because it's marketing framing aimed at someone who is mad.

## Real case

The upload-error review: "check out our new bulk uploader, available to all Pro users" — deferred to a later touchpoint with an owner and a review date; the human-review revision added the type-separation rule above. See `../reviews/upload-error-review.md`.
