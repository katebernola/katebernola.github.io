# Too many options

## What it looks like

A message offering three paths ("You can try again, or check out our new bulk uploader, or contact support"), a screen with five equally-weighted cards, a dialog with three buttons of the same size.

## Why it happens

- Serving every stakeholder's path instead of the user's most likely one.
- Avoiding the responsibility of recommending.

## How to catch it

- Count the verbs offered to the user. More than two is a committee talking.
- At a decision point: more facts can build confidence — or stall the decision.

## The fix

Pick the primary path and recommend it. Put the alternates behind disclosure or a step away. Give the deciding fact, not all facts.

At an error, the stakes double: the user is already frustrated, and options fuel the fire. Always identify the one best next step and point to it — viable and complete, so it can't fail again.
