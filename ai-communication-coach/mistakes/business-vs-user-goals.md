# Business vs user goals

## What it looks like

A "user goal" that reads "discover the new feature" or "become aware of the upgrade." Users don't wake up wanting to adopt, engage, or convert. Those are our goals in their voice.

## Why it happens

- The request arrived from a stakeholder with a target, and the target got restated as a need.
- It's genuinely hard to hear the difference from inside the building.

## How to catch it

- The costume test: would the user say it that way to a friend? A real user goal survives "I just want to…"
- Watch for adopt / engage / discover / upgrade / convert / aware in anything labeled a user goal.

## The fix

Separate them explicitly. Write the user goal in the user's words; put the business goal in its own column, honestly. Then find the overlap: "when the user succeeds at X, the business gets Y." Copy lives in the overlap; everything outside it has to be pushed, and pushed copy sounds pushed.
