# Explaining before action

## What it looks like

An education page before the enrollment button. A tour before the first task. Three paragraphs of context above a form.

## Why it happens

- Stakeholders equate informed with confident ("users need to know X before they can Y").
- Writing explanation feels responsible. Cutting it feels risky.

## How to catch it

- Ask what the user would do differently after reading each explanatory block. "Nothing, but they'd understand" is the tell.
- Check the stage: people in setup want to finish setup.

## The fix

Attach teaching to the action, not ahead of it. Tips read before they matter are tips forgotten. Let the user commit, then teach inside the task — with disclosure available for those who want depth first.

## Real case

Retirement enrollment: eligibility, 401(k) definitions, compound interest, and tax explanations were assumed necessary before enrollment. The strategy cut and deferred most of it. See `../patterns/retirement-enrollment.md`.
