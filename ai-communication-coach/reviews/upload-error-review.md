# Upload error review — 2026-07-18

Generated by running the full 13-step framework and standards engine (workshop prototype) on a sample error message. This was the system's first end-to-end run.

## Context

- Component: error message — shown when a file upload fails partway through.
- User: a marketing manager uploading assets before a launch deadline. Stage: moment of need.
- Request origin: a stakeholder ask.
- User goals: fix a problem; finish the task and move on. Business goals: upsell/upgrade; fewer support tickets.
- Communication goal: reassure, then instruct.

## The draft as it arrived (49 words, 6 messages)

> Oops! Something went wrong with your upload. We're really sorry about that. Please note that files over 2GB are not supported at this time. You can try again, or check out our new bulk uploader which is now available to all Pro users. Your other files were uploaded successfully.

## Findings

**1. The business and user goals pulled in opposite directions.**
Observation: upsell/upgrade as a business goal during a moment of need. Reasoning: upselling someone who is stuck reads as opportunism at their worst moment. Recommendation: serve the user's goal now; give the upsell its own moment. Tradeoffs: defers a measurable conversion for unmeasurable trust. See [marketing-in-product](../mistakes/marketing-in-product.md).

**2. Message verdicts.**
- "Oops!" — cut (user already knows something went wrong; the error state communicates it).
- "Something went wrong with your upload." — keep, lead. Flagged by clarity check: names no cause.
- "We're really sorry about that." — cut.
- "Please note that files over 2GB are not supported at this time." — keep, lead; rewritten.
- "You can try again, or check out our new bulk uploader…" — defer to a later touchpoint; owner: growth team; review date 2026-10-01.
- "Your other files were uploaded successfully." — keep, support (reassurance), behind progressive disclosure.

**3. Standards engine.**
First run: 19 pass / 11 review / 1 fix. Notable: "successfully" pushed one message to a grade-12 reading level; two passive constructions; two time-bound phrases without review dates; "Pro" flagged for product-name confirmation; two lead messages competing for position one (accepted — sequenced instead of merged).

## Outcome — coach's draft (3 messages, 17 words)

1. Something went wrong with your upload. *(lead, inline)*
2. Files over 2GB won't upload. *(second, inline)*
3. Your other files are safe. *(support, helper text)*

After edits: 24 pass / 7 review / 0 fix. Remaining reviews were judgment calls, accepted knowingly.

## Revision — human review, 2026-07-18

A human content designer reviewed the coach's draft and found what the rules missed:

- **The generic wrapper survived.** "Something went wrong with your upload" doesn't need to be said when the specific cause is stated: "Files over 2GB won't upload" communicates the failure itself. The specific problem is its own announcement.
- **The "or" hid two messages.** "Try again, or check out our new bulk uploader" is two separate messages fused into one. The atomicity split missed it. (The engine now flags "or" in longer messages.)
- **"Try again" wasn't viable.** Retrying an over-2GB file fails again. The next step must be complete: reduce the file size, *then* try again.
- **Conditional worth recording:** if the 2GB limit isn't actually the problem this user hit, the limit message doesn't belong at all. Don't explain problems the user doesn't have.
- **The upsell framing, refined.** "Check out our new…" is marketing language at an error — inappropriate when the user is already mad. PLG nuance: on a free tier where the limit is a plan limit, prompting an upgrade can be appropriate. But an upsell and an error are completely different message types needing different treatments — separate messages, never fused, and never confused.
- **One best next step.** Multiple options fuel the fire of frustration. Identify the single best next step and point the user to it.

## Final copy after human review (3 messages, 14 words)

1. Files over 2GB won't upload. *(lead, inline — the problem, specifically)*
2. Reduce the file size and try again. *(next step, inline — viable and complete)*
3. Your other files are safe. *(support, helper text)*

The system lesson: the coach's rules produced discipline; the human review produced correctness. Both passes are the process.
