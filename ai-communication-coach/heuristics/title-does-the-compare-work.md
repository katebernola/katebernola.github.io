# Titles do the compare work

## The idea

When a user faces a choice between options, the titles carry the comparison. Explanations appear one at a time — when an option is selected.

Seeing one explanation feels like "yep, that's what I thought." Seeing two explanations side by side forces the user to compare paragraphs — and that's a nightmare.

## Rules

- Option titles must be clear enough to choose between on their own.
- Explanatory text appears when an option is selected, not before.
- One explanation on screen at a time.
- If the titles can't carry the choice, fix the titles — don't add more explanation.

## Where this came from

The mid-period rate change review (2026-07-19). The two apply options ("Next open check date" vs. "Apply both old and new rates") each needed explanation — but showing both explanations at once would force comparison. Clear titles + one on-selection explanation was the answer.
