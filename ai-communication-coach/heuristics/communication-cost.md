# Communication cost

## Purpose

Every message has a price. This heuristic is for reading the receipt.

## Principles

- Every message spends attention, and attention is the budget the lead message depends on.
- Some messages also spend trust: persuasion at a bad moment, cheerfulness during stress, a trap in a cancel flow. Trust spends are invisible on dashboards.
- Telling people what they already know still bills them.

## Rules

- Price every message before keeping it: attention cost, trust cost, maintenance cost (who keeps it true?).
- Interruption cost is paid before a word is read — notifications and modals start in debt.

## Decision heuristics

- Cutting risks the rare user who needed it; keeping taxes every user who didn't. Compare the sizes of those groups.
- If the business goal must be pushed, it will sound pushed. Pushed copy costs more than it earns.
- The impression you leave when a user quits is the marketing for their return.

## Common mistakes

- Counting only the benefit of a message and none of its costs.
- See `../mistakes/marketing-in-product.md`.

## Examples

- The upsell in the upload error: deferred because its trust cost at a moment of need exceeded its conversion value.

## Counterexamples

- Awaiting one: a message whose interruption cost was clearly worth it.

## Exceptions

- Safety-critical warnings are allowed to overspend attention. That is what the budget is being saved for.
