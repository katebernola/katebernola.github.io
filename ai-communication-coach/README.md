# Alpha

Alpha is a communication decision coach: a knowledge base for making communication decisions in product design — what to say, where, how loud, and (most often) what not to say at all.

It powers a coaching practice, not a chatbot: a structured decision process that runs *before* any UI copy gets written, challenges assumptions the way a senior content designer would, and frames every recommendation as observation → reasoning → recommendation → tradeoffs.

## How it's organized

- `books/` — enduring knowledge. Principles, the decision framework, standards, governance. Numbered in reading order.
- `patterns/` — solved problems. Real case studies with the request, the assumptions, the strategy, and the lessons.
- `components/` — copy rules per UI component (banner, dialog, tooltip …).
- `terminology/` — approved words, banned words, product language.
- `heuristics/` — fast rules of thumb. Small files, one idea each.
- `mistakes/` — named recurring failures, so critiques can point at them.
- `beliefs/` — things held true with a stated confidence level, open to challenge.
- `reviews/` — saved critiques. Every generated review lands here and becomes future reference material.
- `examples/` — curated before/afters and teaching examples.
- `thinking/` — raw ideas, open questions, hypotheses. Not settled. Not standards.
- `prototypes/` — working software built from this knowledge.
